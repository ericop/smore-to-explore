Smore to Explore

Printable Rulebook

Build the best campground over three summers by expanding your land, hiring the right contractors, and scoring the goals that matter this season. Each player builds a separate board, but everyone drafts from the same public market.

2-5 Players Pass And Play 8 x 5 Board Per Player Three Summers

2. Game Overview

Each player is designing a campground people actually want to visit. Over three summers you grow roads, forests, scenic spaces, and water parcels, then build campsites, lodging, and amenities that fit that land.

The game supports 2 to 5 players. The code uses the same board size, money, and setup for every supported player count.

3. Setup

  1. Give each player a board, $100,000, and the same 10 starting landscape tiles.
  2. Shuffle the Early, Mid, Late, and Camp Director goal decks separately.
  3. Reveal 4 Early Summer goals.
  4. Keep the Camp Director deck face down.
  5. Fill the contractor market with 2 amenity columns and 4 camp columns, each 8 spaces tall.
  6. Decide player order.

4. How A Summer Works

  1. Landscape Tile Phase: place every landscape tile in your current hand.
  2. Market Tile Phase: buy from the contractor market, place tiles, or pass.
  3. Goal Scoring Phase: score the 4 active public goals for the current Summer.

After Early Summer, reveal 3 Camp Director goals. After Mid Summer and Late Summer, every player also receives $50,000 and 8 new landscape tiles before the next Landscape Tile Phase begins.

5. Landscape Tile Phase

Landscape tiles create the foundation of your campground. You cannot build campsites or amenities on a square until that square already has a landscape tile.

Legal Placement

  • Stay inside the 8 x 5 board.
  • Do not overlap another landscape tile.
  • Your first tile must be the Entrance.
  • The Entrance gate must face the outside edge of the board.
  • Every later tile must touch your current campground orthogonally.
  • Road edges must meet road edges.
  • Open edges cannot crash into road edges.
  • By the end of the phase, the Camp Office must connect to the Entrance by road.

6. Market Tile Phase

During the Market Tile Phase, players take turns hiring contractors from the shared market.

Buying From The Market

  • Every market tile costs $10,000.
  • If you select a deeper tile in a column, you buy every tile above it too.
  • You pay for the full stack immediately.
  • You must place the stack from top to bottom.
  • If you have not placed any tile from that stack yet, you may cancel the purchase.
  • If every player passes, the Summer ends.

Big Market Items

These always occupy exactly 2 squares: RV Site with Full Hookups, Group Site, Waterfront Site, Horse Riding, and Event Pavilion.

Important A big market item is not legal unless both occupied squares are legal.

7. Goal Scoring Phase

At the end of each Summer, every player checks the 4 active public goals for that season. If a player satisfies a goal, that player scores it. After Early Summer, reveal 3 Camp Director goals. Those 3 final goals score after Late Summer.

Example: If Eliana has 3 Rustic Tent Forest tiles at the end of Early Summer and Tents in the Pines is active, she scores it. Eric and Chris may also score it if they built the same thing.

8. Detailed Tile Placement Rules

Use this reference when a tile's short market text is not enough.

Sites

Rustic Tent Forest: almost any open parcel. Helps rustic, variety, and tent goals.

Tent Site with Electric Hookup: needs at least 1 road edge. Helps electric, comfort, and variety goals.

RV Site with Full Hookups: big market item. Both halves need at least 2 road edges. Helps premium and RV goals.

Group Site: big market item. Both halves need at least 1 road edge. Helps family and group goals.

Cabin: needs at least 1 road edge. Helps premium and comfort goals.

Waterfront Site: big market item. At least 1 half must be on water-edge terrain. Helps premium and waterfront goals.

Amenities And Attractions

Horse Riding: big market item. Both halves need road access and scenic, forest, or board-edge terrain.

Firewood: practical road access or scenic support.

Pool: at least 1 road edge.

Bike Rental: at least 1 road edge.

Canoe Rental: must be on a water-edge parcel.

Event Pavilion: big market item. Both halves need at least 1 road edge.

Hiking Trail: scenic, forest, or board-edge terrain.

Ice Cream Vending: at least 1 road edge.

Playground: at least 1 road edge.

Bathrooms: at least 2 road edges.

Important Big market items count as one tile for goals that ask how many of that type you have, but both occupied squares matter for range, adjacency, and placement legality.

9. End Of Game And Final Scoring

  1. Score the 4 active Late Summer goals.
  2. Score the 3 revealed Camp Director goals.
  3. Add those points to each player's total.
  4. Highest total score wins.

The code shows total score, money left, and director points in the final standings, but it breaks ties by score only. No further tie breaker is coded.

10. Quick Reference

Setup Checklist

  • boards out
  • $100,000 each
  • 10 start landscape tiles each
  • 4 Early goals revealed
  • market filled 6 x 8
  • player order chosen

Placement Checklist

  • inside the board
  • no overlap
  • landscape first, market second
  • reserved Entrance and Office stay empty of camp tiles
  • big items need 2 legal squares
  • Waterfront needs at least 1 water-edge square

Game End Checklist

  • score active Late Summer goals
  • score revealed Camp Director goals
  • highest total score wins

Rules Needing Confirmation

  • Finite physical supply counts: the app uses weighted refills rather than a shrinking deck.
  • Expansion landscape mix: the app draws by weighted recipe, not fixed print count.
  • Tie breaker: final code sorts by score only.
  • Physical money and score components: tracked digitally in code, not specified as printed parts.

Suggested tie-breaker for tabletop testing: most money left, then most Camp Director points, then shared victory.

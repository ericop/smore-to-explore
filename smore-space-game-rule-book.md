# Smore to Explore Rule Book

## Overview

**Smore to Explore** is a campground-building tile placement game for **2 to 5 players**.

Over the course of **3 rounds** of summer play:

- **Early Summer**
- **Mid Summer**
- **Late Summer**

players build out their own campground by:

1. laying landscape tiles to create the road and terrain skeleton,
2. buying camp tiles from a shared contractor market,
3. placing those camp tiles onto valid landscape spaces, and
4. scoring seasonal objectives plus longer-term Camp Director objectives.

The winner is the player with the most points after Late Summer scoring and final Camp Director scoring.

---

## Theme

You are designing a campground people actually want to visit.

Some guests want rustic tent camping.  
Some want electric hookups or RV access.  
Some want premium cabins, waterfront spaces, and canoe rentals.  
Some just want ice cream and somewhere for the kids to burn off all the marshmallow sugar.

Your job is to build the most appealing, practical, and well-planned campground by the end of the season.

---

## Players

- **2 to 5 players**
- Best with **3 or 4** if playing in person for the first time

## Components

### Per Player

Each player needs their own:

- **1 campground board** with an **8 columns by 5 rows** grid
- **$100,000 starting money**
- **10 starting landscape tiles**
  - 1 Entrance to Camp tile
  - 1 Camp Office tile
  - 4 Straight Road tiles
  - 2 Road Turn tiles
  - 1 Four-Way Cross tile
  - 1 Three-Way Split tile

### Shared Components

- **Contractor market** with **6 columns**, each **8 tiles tall**
- Camp tile supply or deck
- Objective cards for:
  - Early Summer
  - Mid Summer
  - Late Summer
  - Camp Director
- Money supply
- Score tracker or paper
- Optional first player marker

### Additional Landscape Expansion Tiles

Between rounds, players gain **8 more landscape tiles** from an expansion pool.

Recommended expansion pool includes a mix of:

- Straight Road
- Road Turn
- Three-Way Split
- Four-Way Cross
- Forest Nook
- Scenic Meadow
- Lakeside Road
- Lakeside Turn
- Lakeside Meadow

---

## Tile Types

There are **2 main tile layers** in the game.

### 1. Landscape Tiles

Landscape tiles form the foundation of the campground.

They create:

- road access
- scenic parcels
- forest parcels
- waterfront parcels
- usable spaces where camp tiles may later be built

A landscape tile must be placed before a camp tile can be built on that space.

### 2. Camp Tiles

Camp tiles represent campsites, attractions, and support amenities.

These are placed **on top of** landscape tiles.

You may only have:

- **1 landscape tile per board space**
- **1 camp tile per board space**

---

## Board and Geography

Each player's campground is an **8 wide by 5 high grid**.

For the physical game, it is strongly recommended that the board use these outer geography cues:

- **top edge** = lake / water side
- **left edge** = woods
- **right edge** = open field
- **bottom edge** = main arrival road / highway edge

This helps scenic and waterfront placements make sense.

### Board Space Terms

Each square on the board is called a **parcel**.

A parcel may hold:

- no tile
- a landscape tile only
- a landscape tile plus a camp tile

---

## Landscape Tile Reference

### Entrance to Camp
- Must be placed on a board edge
- Its gate side must face off the board
- It is the anchor of the campground
- It starts the road network

### Camp Office
- Must connect into the road network
- Cannot later hold a camp tile
- Counts as an important building for some objectives

### Straight Road
- Road exits on 2 opposite sides

### Road Turn
- Road exits on 2 adjacent sides

### Four-Way Cross
- Road exits on all 4 sides

### Three-Way Split
- Road exits on 3 sides

### Scenic Meadow
- Open parcel with no required road
- Good for flexible placement

### Forest Nook
- Scenic forest parcel
- Good for rustic camping and hiking

### Lakeside Road
- Includes a water edge plus road access
- Good for waterfront builds

### Lakeside Turn
- A lakeside road tile with a turn

### Lakeside Meadow
- Scenic waterfront parcel with no built road

---

## Camp Tile Reference

All camp tiles cost **$10,000 each** unless you later rebalance the game.

### Campsites and Lodging

#### Rustic Tent Forest
- Flexible rustic camping
- Best on forest or scenic parcels

#### Tent Site with Electric Hookup
- Needs at least 1 road edge on the parcel below

#### RV Site with Full Hookups
- Needs strong road access
- Recommended rule: at least **2 road edges** on the parcel below

#### Group Site
- Large social campsite
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Likes nearby amenities and intersections

#### Cabin
- Premium lodging
- Needs at least 1 road edge

#### Waterfront Site
- Premium lodging
- Must be placed on a parcel with a **water edge**

#### Horse Riding
- Rare activity tile
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Best on scenic, forest, or border parcels

### Amenities

#### Firewood
- Flexible support amenity
- Best near tents and group sites

#### Pool
- Family amenity
- Needs at least 1 road edge

#### Bike Rental
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Best on a long connected road network

#### Canoe Rental
- Must be placed on a parcel with a **water edge**

#### Event Pavilion
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Best near clusters of campsites

#### Hiking Trail
- Best on scenic, forest, or edge parcels

#### Ice Cream Vending
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Best near central traffic

#### Playground
- Needs at least 1 road edge
- Best near family camping and group sites

#### Bathrooms
- Needs strong road access
- Recommended rule: at least **2 road edges**
- Serves as a practical support amenity

## Game Flow

The game lasts **3 rounds**:

1. Early Summer
2. Mid Summer
3. Late Summer

Each round has these phases:

1. **Landscape Phase**
2. **Build Phase**
3. **Scoring Phase**
4. **Round Transition**

---

## Round 1: Starting Landscape Phase

Before any player buys camp tiles, every player must place their **10 starting landscape tiles**.

### Starting Landscape Placement Rules

A landscape tile may be placed only if all of the following are true:

- it is inside the 8×5 board
- it does not overlap another landscape tile
- if it has a road edge touching another placed tile, that neighboring edge must also be a road
- an open edge may not crash into a road edge on an adjacent tile
- after the first tile, every newly placed landscape tile must touch at least 1 existing landscape tile
- the first tile placed should be the **Entrance**
- the Entrance tile’s gate must face the outer edge of the board
- road tiles with road edges should connect into the existing road network as they are placed

### Finished Setup Requirements

A player has completed setup only when:

- all 10 starting landscape tiles are placed
- the landscape layout is one connected arrangement
- the Entrance exists
- the Camp Office exists
- the Camp Office is connected to the Entrance by road

Once all players have completed setup, the game moves to the Build Phase.

---

## Expansion Landscape Phase in Later Rounds

At the start of Mid Summer and Late Summer, each player gains:

- **$50,000**
- **8 new landscape tiles**

These new landscape tiles must be placed before that player may buy more camp tiles in the new round.

### Expansion Placement Rules
Use the same placement rules as setup, with these additions:

- new landscape tiles must touch the existing campground
- the overall layout must remain connected
- roads should extend logically
- scenic and lakeside parcels may be added to support future scoring

---

## Build Phase

During the Build Phase, players take turns buying and placing **1 camp tile per turn**.

### On Your Turn

1. Choose **1 face-up tile** from the contractor market
2. Pay **$10,000**
3. Place that tile on a valid parcel in your campground
4. Refill the empty market space from the matching pool
5. End your turn

Play continues clockwise.

### Optional Pass Rule
If desired for the tabletop version, a player may pass when they do not want to buy any more camp tiles that round.

If all players pass in sequence, the round ends immediately and proceeds to scoring.

If you want a tighter game, do **not** use passing and instead play a fixed number of turns per round. See the Unclear Rules section.

---

## Camp Tile Placement Rules

A camp tile may be placed only if:

- the parcel already has a landscape tile
- the parcel does not already have a camp tile
- the parcel is not the Entrance
- the parcel is not the Camp Office
- you can pay the cost
- the tile’s own placement rule is satisfied

### Access Rules Summary

#### Needs at least 1 road edge on the parcel below
- Tent Site with Electric Hookup
- Group Site
- Cabin
- Pool
- Bike Rental
- Event Pavilion
- Ice Cream Vending
- Playground

#### Needs at least 2 road edges on the parcel below
- RV Site with Full Hookups
- Bathrooms

#### Needs a water-edge parcel
- Waterfront Site
- Canoe Rental

#### Needs scenic / forest / border support
- Horse Riding
- Hiking Trail

#### Flexible but thematic
- Rustic Tent Forest
- Firewood

### Practical Access Principle
If a rule feels unclear during tabletop play, use this common-sense test:

> Would a real camper reasonably be able to access this feature from the road network or terrain shown?

If the answer is clearly no, the placement is illegal.

---

## Market Rules

The contractor market always shows:

- **6 columns**
- **8 visible tiles in each column**

Suggested structure:

- Column 1: Amenity
- Column 2: Amenity
- Column 3: Camp
- Column 4: Camp
- Column 5: Camp
- Column 6: Camp

### Refill Rule
Whenever a tile is bought, immediately refill that market space from the matching tile pool.

If a matching pool is empty, leave the space empty.

---

## Objectives

There are 2 kinds of objectives.

### 1. Shared Seasonal Objectives
These are tied to the current round.
They are face up and all players may try to satisfy them.

### 2. Camp Director Objectives
These unlock after the first round scoring.
They represent long-term management goals and usually score at the end of the game.

---

## Seasonal Objective Timing

### Early Summer
At game start, reveal **4 Early Summer objectives**.

### Mid Summer
At the start of Mid Summer, reveal **4 Mid Summer objectives**.

### Late Summer
At the start of Late Summer, reveal **4 Late Summer objectives**.

### Camp Director Objectives
After Early Summer scoring, reveal **3 Camp Director objectives** that remain active through the rest of the game.

---

## Scoring Phase

At the end of each round:

1. Check the round’s shared objectives
2. Each player scores points for any completed objectives
3. Record the score
4. If this was the end of Early Summer, reveal Camp Director objectives
5. If this was not Late Summer, give each player:
   - $50,000
   - 8 new landscape tiles
6. Begin the next round

At the end of Late Summer:

1. Score Late Summer shared objectives
2. Score Camp Director objectives
3. Highest total score wins

### Ties
If tied, use these tie-breakers in order:

1. most remaining money
2. most completed objectives total
3. longest connected road network
4. shared victory

---

## Recommended Turn Structure for 2 to 5 Players

Because the digital prototype is currently single-player, the tabletop version needs a clean multiplayer turn structure.

Use this recommended structure:

### Landscape Phases
All players complete their own landscape placement before the next build phase begins.

You may either:
- let players do this one at a time in turn order, or
- let everyone do it simultaneously if your group prefers lower downtime

### Build Phases
Take turns clockwise, one purchase and one placement per turn.

### Round End Trigger
Use one of these:

#### Option A: Pass Trigger
The round ends when all players pass in sequence.

#### Option B: Fixed Turn Count
For a more predictable game length, each player takes:
- **4 build turns in Early Summer**
- **4 build turns in Mid Summer**
- **4 build turns in Late Summer**

Either option works. Pick one before the game begins.

---

## Recommended Tabletop Clarifications for Objective Scoring

Use these definitions consistently:

### Adjacent
Orthogonally adjacent only:
- up
- down
- left
- right

Diagonal does not count unless a card says otherwise.

### Nearby
Within **2 orthogonal steps** unless the card says otherwise.

### Connected to the Main Road
A tile is connected to the main road if its parcel is part of the same reachable road network as the Entrance.

### Central Area
The center zone of the board is:
- rows 2 through 4 if counting from 1
- columns 3 through 6 if counting from 1

### Premium Tile
A premium tile is any tile with a luxury or premium feel, such as:
- RV Site with Full Hookups
- Cabin
- Waterfront Site
- Pool
- Horse Riding
- Ice Cream Vending

### Campsite or Lodging Tile
These count as guest-stay spaces:
- Rustic Tent Forest
- Tent Site with Electric Hookup
- RV Site with Full Hookups
- Group Site
- Cabin
- Waterfront Site

Amenities do not count unless a card says otherwise.

---

## Tabletop Objective Resolution Guidance

If an objective checks something slightly fuzzy, use the following guidelines:

- **road length** = number of connected road parcels in that route
- **road hub** = a road parcel with 3 or more connected road exits
- **dead-end road** = a road parcel with only 1 road connection
- **road loop** = a closed road circuit
- **cluster** = orthogonally connected group of matching camp tiles
- **balanced layout** = built camp tiles spread across all 4 board quadrants
- **unused supported spaces** = landscape parcels that could legally hold a camp tile but are still empty

If a specific objective card needs a more exact interpretation, write it on the card sleeve or reference sheet before starting.

## Example Round Sequence

### Early Summer Example
1. All players place their 10 starting landscape tiles
2. Reveal 4 Early Summer objectives
3. Players take turns buying and placing camp tiles
4. Everyone passes or the fixed turn count is reached
5. Score Early Summer objectives
6. Reveal 3 Camp Director objectives
7. Each player gains $50,000 and 8 expansion landscape tiles

### Mid Summer Example
1. Players place their 8 new landscape tiles
2. Reveal 4 Mid Summer objectives
3. Players take turns buying and placing camp tiles
4. Score Mid Summer objectives
5. Each player gains $50,000 and 8 more landscape tiles

### Late Summer Example
1. Players place their final 8 landscape tiles
2. Reveal 4 Late Summer objectives
3. Players take turns buying and placing camp tiles
4. Score Late Summer objectives
5. Score Camp Director objectives
6. Determine winner

## Winning the Game

After Late Summer:

1. score Late Summer objectives
2. score all revealed Camp Director objectives
3. total each player’s points

The highest score wins and becomes the campground legend most likely to get a five-star review that says, “great trail access, kids loved the playground, bathrooms were suspiciously convenient.”

## Unclear Rules / Needs Decision

These are the main things that should be explicitly locked down before calling the tabletop game fully finished.

### 1. Is the game truly competitive multiplayer or mostly parallel multiplayer?
The current digital structure is effectively single-board, single-player logic per game state.
For tabletop, this rulebook assumes:
- each player has their own campground board
- all players compete on score
- all players use the same shared market and objective rows

This is the cleanest multiplayer version, but it should be formally declared.

### 2. Exactly how many build turns happen each round?
Right now the prototype supports buy-and-place flow, but a multiplayer tabletop version needs a hard round-ending rule.

You should choose one:
- all players pass in sequence to end the round
- each player gets a fixed number of turns per round

I recommend:
- 4 turns per player per round for first testing

### 3. Are landscape phases simultaneous or turn-based?
Both work.

Recommended:
- simultaneous for experienced groups
- turn-based for first plays and easier rules enforcement

### 4. Are market tiles finite or effectively infinite?
The current prototype behaves like a refill system from weighted pools rather than strict depletion tracking.

For tabletop, decide:
- **finite supply** with true depletion, or
- **refill from a shuffled deck / bag**

I recommend:
- finite supply for camp tiles
- random refill from a shuffled stack per market category

### 5. Are all 4 seasonal objectives scored by every player, or only first-to-complete?
This rulebook assumes:
- every player may score every revealed shared objective once

That is clean and friendly, but it should be confirmed.

### 6. Are Camp Director objectives public or drafted?
The current flow suggests revealed active objectives.

This rulebook assumes:
- 3 Camp Director objectives are revealed face up after Early Summer
- all players may score them at game end

If you want more differentiation, each player could instead draft one private director goal.

### 7. Can a player choose not to place all 8 expansion landscape tiles?
The current prototype requires all expansion tiles to be placed before the build phase reopens.

This rulebook follows that same assumption:
- yes, all granted landscape tiles must be placed

That is neat for the digital flow, but it may feel restrictive in tabletop play.

### 8. Do scenic and waterfront parcels depend only on tile art, or also on board edge?
The prototype logic mixes tile tags and board-edge ideas.
For tabletop, this should be explicit.

Recommended rule:
- only the tile itself determines whether it is scenic, forest, or waterfront
- the printed board edge is thematic support, not a mechanical shortcut

### 9. Does adjacency ever count diagonally?
This rulebook says no.
If any future objective wants diagonal counting, it must say so explicitly.

### 10. How are longest road and loops scored if there is ambiguity?
For clean play:
- a road route counts through connected road edges only
- longest road is the size of the largest connected road network or longest valid traced path, depending on the objective wording

You should pick one consistent interpretation and add it to the objective appendix.

### 11. What counts as a “supported empty parcel” for Polished Grounds style objectives?
This rulebook uses:
- any non-Entrance, non-Office landscape parcel that could plausibly hold a camp tile

But that can get fuzzy with specialized requirements.
You may want a stricter definition:
- any non-reserved landscape parcel, regardless of whether a specific tile currently fits there

### 12. Should Bathrooms be a permanent core tile?
The current prototype includes Bathrooms for practical clarity.
If the final theme feels too cluttered, decide whether:
- Bathrooms stay as a full amenity type
- Bathrooms become part of another service tile
- Bathrooms are removed entirely

### 13. Should players be allowed to rotate camp tiles?
Camp tiles are currently conceptually square overlays with no directional edge logic.
This rulebook assumes:
- camp tile rotation does not matter
- only landscape tile rotation matters

### 14. Should players be able to rearrange landscape tiles during setup before finalizing?
Recommended rule:
- yes, during setup only
- once a player says setup is complete, those tiles are locked

For the digital version, placements are immediate, but tabletop play benefits from this flexibility.

### 15. Is there interaction beyond racing the market?
This rulebook assumes:
- no direct sabotage
- no stealing tiles from an opponent’s board
- competition comes from score racing and shared market drafting

That keeps the game cozy instead of “campground zoning hearing with swords.”
